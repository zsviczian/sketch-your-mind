What are they selling in this store?

What's the difference?

Build a Visual Thinking System You Can Trust

What if your notes could do more than just sit there? What if they could spark creativity, reveal connections, and grow with you—without the chaos of scattered sketches or the weight of endless text?

Sketch Your Mind is your guide to visual thinking—not just to draw ideas, but to structure, store, and connect them in a way that makes knowledge easy to find and use.

For too long, we've crammed our thinking into bullet points and walls of words. But your brain doesn't work that way. It thrives on patterns, loops, and space.

This isn’t just another book about sketching notes—it’s a complete visual system for thinking clearly, capturing insights, and building a connected knowledge base that actually works. No hacks, no fluff—just a dynamic, reusable framework that turns your ideas into playful, powerful tools.

Think in Space, Not Just Lines

"Ambidextrous thinking" means combining words and visuals—using sketches to spark clarity, text to ground ideas, and feedback loops to refine them. This isn't about artistic skill. It's about structuring your thoughts so they become visible, tangible, and useful.

A Visual Thinking Framework for Obsidian Users

If you’re an Obsidian user, you already understand the power of linking ideas, building knowledge over time, and structuring your thinking in a system that reflects your mind, not someone else’s.

Sketch Your Mind builds directly on this foundation.

It integrates the best of what we know from Zettelkasten, Building a Second Brain, LYT, and the broader PKM world, then brings in the missing piece: visual thinking.
The result is a multimodal, creative, and flexible approach to PKM that feels native to how we actually think and work.

Let me share how this framework shows up in my own practice, and what you’ll take away from the book.

How I Use These Skills – and Why They Matter in Obsidian

1. Better Presentations and Clearer Communication

In my day job leading architecture teams, clarity is currency.
We’re constantly making complex decisions and aligning people from different domains.

The thinking we do in our notes and modeling tools has to be turned into clear, shareable output.

That’s where visual thinking and storytelling changes the game.
Instead of slides with endless bullet points, I build slides and system diagrams that follow narrative structure and visual logic. One idea per frame. Clear flow. Stakeholder-friendly.

Even for many of my corporate presentations the groundwork happens inside Obsidian.
My ideas live in linked notes. The visuals are often drafted in Excalidraw. The communication flows out of the same system where I think.

But even if I use enterprise modeling tools and typicall enterprise office apps, I apply the same skills, flow and approach as outlined in the book.

2. Understanding, Organizing, and Remembering What I Read

Obsidian gives me the tools to build a map.

When I read a book, I often distill it into a Book-on-a-Page sketch.
That sketch becomes the entry point into deeper layers of understanding: linked atomic notes, back-of-the-card insights, idea blocks.
I use techniques from Buzan’s mind mapping, from sketchnoting, and from the hybrid note format I outline in the book.

These visuals aren’t decorative.
They give structure to complexity. They surface relationships that are hard to see in lists. They help me remember and resurface.

Obsidian’s powerful features make all this scalable.
The Sketch Your Mind approach helps you design this process more intentionally.

3. Playfulness, Creativity, and Flow

Let’s be honest: some parts of PKM can feel heavy.
We chase structure, consistency, atomicity... and sometimes lose the spark.

Visual thinking brings play back into the process.

Sketching in Excalidraw feels different from typing into a text box.
It invites exploration. I’m more willing to make a mess, to follow hunches, to take notes that don’t yet “fit.”
It’s like switching from laying train tracks to building with LEGO: more modular, more forgiving, more fun.

This playfulness leads to deeper engagement.
I stay longer with the material. I experiment more. And because I enjoy the process, I return to it more often.

What Sketch Your Mind Adds

This isn’t just another take on note-taking.
It’s a synthesis of multiple traditions—brought together through the lens of visual thinking and made actionable inside your Obsidian workflow.

Sketch Your Mind builds on:

  • Zettelkasten ideas, as taught by people like Bob Dotto and Sönke Ahrens
  • Linking and progressive summarization from Tiago Forte’s BASB
  • Note-making vs. note-taking insights from Nick Milo and LYT
  • Visual thinking traditions from Tony Buzan, Dan Roam, Dave Gray, Sunni Brown
  • Storytelling tools from Storyworthy by Matthew Dicks and others

And it integrates all of this into:

  • LEGO-style modular thinking
    Break complex topics into small, reusable visual idea blocks.
  • Concept visual frameworks
    A 7-step visual grammar to turn insights into clear, structured sketches.
  • Hybrid notes
    A two-sided format: visual front in Excalidraw, deep synthesis on the textual back.
  • Visual Zettelkasten
    Not just linking notes, but seeing them in space—building a visual map of your knowledge landscape.

Why This Matters to Obsidian Users

I’ve been building in Obsidian for over three years.
My plugins, including Excalidraw and ExcaliBrain, have always aimed to expand what’s possible in visual PKM.

But this book isn’t a how-to manual for plugins.
It’s a thinking system, designed with Obsidian users in mind, but grounded in timeless principles.

If you’re using Obsidian to learn better, create more, or think more clearly—this book will show you a new dimension to that practice.

You’ll learn to:

  • Combine visuals with your existing text-based notes
  • Build notes that work like index cards, visual maps, and creative drafts all in one
  • Create a knowledge system that’s navigable by both links and layout
  • Bring storytelling, creativity, and visual structure into your daily workflow
