What Good Models Do

How simple hand‑drawn models make systems visible, spark insight, and guide better conversations.

Abstract

In this session Doug Neill shares practical ways to sketch models that clarify complex ideas and invite participation. We will explore two tools: the What Good Models Do framework for designing effective visuals and the “Done” Diagnostic (Glance, Talk, and Audience tests) for finishing and stress‑testing your sketches.

  • Make systems visible with clear, minimal marks.
  • Use the “Done” Diagnostic to refine contrast, flow, and engagement.
  • Turn notes into models that prompt action and conversation.

About the Author

Doug Neill

Doug helps people make sense of the world by making marks on the page. After training as a teacher, he founded Verbal to Visual, where he teaches simple drawing and diagramming for learning, problem‑solving, and storytelling. He shares this work through hand‑sketched videos, online courses, and live workshops. Skills include visual thinking, curriculum design, creative problem solving, and video production.
